Form

If you sell jewelry, you will need sales and contact forms to interact with your customers as they select and place goods into shopping carts and perhaps contact you for customer service. That will require the use of a third file or linked section called Javascript, which enables the code to create actions on the website. Here are some important forms which can be placed in the HTML and Javascript (JS) files.

Buttons

Animated Buttons - "Pressed Effect"

Buttons are useful in allowing a user to navigate his or her way around the website, as well as submit orders, and other purposes you can think of. They can be styled large or small, customized to your specifications, and will have HTML, JS and CSS components, to be placed by class in each page. Add class="smBtn", class="mdBtn" or class="lgBtn".

Helpers

Helper Classes improve the user experience even more, by enhancing the look of your website.

Floats allow us to move a picture into the right place on our website. It’s possible to position it left or right on our CSS file. Some examples are below.

Image floats left

Place the class=”float-left” code into your image’s <div> container, and your picture floats to the left, while your text stays on the right.

Image floats right

You can do the same thing with the class=”float-right” which will place your image to the right

No-print

If you want to hide some elements in a printed version from your website, add class="no-print" into your HTML

ACCORDION

The accordion feature is frequently used in e-commerce, enabling a buyer to click an image to see information about the same product. It’s immediate, it’s easy, and buyers often expect it. Add class="accordion" to the button and class="accordion-panel" to the container which you want to show up after clicking the button.
